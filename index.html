🌊 How to Stop Microplastic Pollution & Protect Aquatic Life

🚫 Reduce Single-Use Plastics

  • Avoid single-use plastic cups, water bottles, bags, cutlery, and straws.
  • Incorporate reusable alternatives made from stainless steel, glass, or cotton fabrics.
  • Encourage companies and restaurants to offer plastic-free bio-degradable packaging.

🧹 Proper Waste Disposal

  • Never flush cosmetic products containing plastic microbeads or micro-powders down drains.
  • Dispose of synthetic ropes, lines, and commercial fishing nets properly to avoid ghost nets.
  • Participate actively in river, beach, or community clean-ups to intercept trash early.

🐟 Protect Aquatic Habitats

  • Microplastics easily enter fish tissue, leading to structural bioaccumulation up the food chain.
  • Help prevent urban runoff from carrying synthetic tire dust and microfibers directly into waterways.
  • Support marine sanctuary designations and native seagrass restoration programs.

🔬 Support Detection Technology

  • Real-time optical diagnostic sensors (like this ESP32 UNO system) isolate microplastic signals locally.
  • Continuous water-quality mapping lets scientific teams trace and isolate pollution sources rapidly.
  • Low-cost, scalable open hardware empowers citizen scientists to monitor neighborhood watersheds.
